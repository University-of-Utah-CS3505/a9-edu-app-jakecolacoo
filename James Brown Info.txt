James Brown, known as “The Godfather of Soul,” was a significant figure in the 1960s music scene. He is widely considered the founding father of funk, and his musical style influenced R&B, jazz, and rock. His innovations in the funk genre had a profound musical influence for years to come, laying a rhythmic foundation for everything from disco to hip-hop. His music became anthems for the civil rights movement, making him a seminal socio-political figure. His influence on music and culture continues to resonate today.

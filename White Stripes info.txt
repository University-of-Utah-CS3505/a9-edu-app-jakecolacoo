The White Stripes was an American rock duo that significantly influenced the sound of alternative rock in the 2000s. Their unique blend of punk, blues, and folk music, combined with their simple, powerful sound and raw live performances resonated with listeners and musicians alike, defining the sound of 21st-century rock. Their breakthrough album, “White Blood Cells,” and the single “Fell in Love with a Girl” became media darlings, propelling them to the forefront of the garage rock revival scene. Their single “Seven Nation Army,” with its distinctive guitar riff, became one of their most recognizable songs and is hummed by fans at sports stadiums to this day.

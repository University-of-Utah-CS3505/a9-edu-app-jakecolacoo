Louis Armstrong, a virtuoso cornet and trumpet player, rose to prominence in the 1920s. He was a protege of King Oliver and became one of the most loved musicians of the era. Armstrong’s influence in jazz was foundational, shifting the music’s focus from collective improvisation to solo performance. He was praised for his sharp sense of harmony and his ability to time his inflections. Armstrong’s improvisations permanently altered the landscape of jazz, making the improvising soloist the focal point of the performance. His influence extended far beyond jazz, impacting soloists in every genre of American popular music.

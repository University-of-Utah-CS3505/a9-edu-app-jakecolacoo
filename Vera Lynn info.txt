Vera Lynn, known as "the Forces' Sweetheart," was an important musical voice during the Second World War in the 1940s. Her song "We'll Meet Again," filled with soaring optimism and poignant nostalgia, became one of the first great pop hits. It resonated deeply with servicemen, who voted Lynn their favorite musical artist. Her music, broadcast to soldiers fighting abroad, symbolized love and longing. Her wholesome stage persona and sentimental material endeared her to the public of her time.
